Identify Indicators

Some indicators could include: food insecurity, clothing or housing needs, physical or mental health issues, addiction, or withdrawal from the community.

  • You can’t help someone if you don’t know they are struggling. In addition to physical indicators, subtle indicators such as consistent tiredness, social withdrawal, and less engagement with faith activities can signal that someone needs help.
  • Be discreet, respectful, and judgment-free when approaching someone to see if they need help—all people have, and deserve, dignity.

Address Urgent Needs

Help provide or connect people to food, clothing, shelter, medical care, addiction help, community connection, and other immediate needs.

  • It is impossible for someone to change if their immediate needs are not being met.
  • Use congregational resources such as direct financial assistance and food banks.
  • Contact appropriate outside resources from other faiths, nonprofits, and government organizations—a list is available on the resources page.

Connect with Mentor

Establish a relationship and/or connect them with a mentor.

  • As a mentor, avoid “fixing” problems—there will always be more. Instead, help people learn to solve their own problems.
  • Mentors build a relationship with the individual, share faith principles that encourage self-reliance, help set and support goals with the individual, and help the individual access congregational and community resources.

Shift Mindsets

Build motivation for long-term sustainable change by using spiritual resources to shape a clear vision and higher purpose.

  • Mentors share spiritual principles that encourage self-reliant thinking. These should be adapted based on the faith of the individual.
  • Some Christian examples include the inherent value of the human soul, God’s desire for us to become more like Him, the enabling power of Christ to help us do hard things, accountability to God, our responsibility to our families, and the importance of serving others.

Address Challenges

Work toward removing impediments such as addiction, health, or legal challenges.

  • There are many challenges that people in poverty face, and community resources are often the best way to address them.
  • This can take time and patience, so make sure that the individual receives encouragement and keeps a clear vision.

Develop Skills

Build skills and assets, such as: increasing education, gaining certifications, developing job skills, practicing financial management, and consistent goal setting.

  • Skills and resources are vital to self-reliance; these are tools that help someone provide for their needs and wants.
  • Community mentors, resources, and events are the best way to build skills and resources.
